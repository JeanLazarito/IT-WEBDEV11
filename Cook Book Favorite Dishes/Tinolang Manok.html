T I N O L A N G  M A N O K

Tinolang manok is a traditional Filipino chicken soup that features tender chicken pieces simmered with ginger, garlic, and onions in a light, savory broth. The dish often includes green papaya or chayote, along with leafy greens like spinach or malunggay. The result is a comforting, aromatic soup with a delicate flavor, perfect for a nourishing meal.



INGREDIENTS



  • 2 lbs. Chicken cut into serving pieces
  • 1 Cup malunggay leaves
  • 1 Cup hot pepper leaves
  • 1/8 Teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 Piece unripe papaya (wedged)
  • 6 Cups water
  • 1 Piece Knorr chicken cube (sliced)
  • 1 Piece onion (sliced)
  • 4 Cloves garlic (crushed and choppped)
  • 3 Thumbs ginger (julienne)
  • 2 Tablespoons fish sauce
  • 3 Tablespoons vegetable oil


INSTRUCTIONS



  • 1Heat oil in a pot.
  • 2Sauté garlic, onion, and ginger. Add the ground black pepper.
  • 3When the onion starts to get soft, add the chicken. Cook for 5 minutes or until it turns light brown.
  • 4Pour the water. Let boil. Cover and then set the heat to low. Boil for 40 minutes.
  • 5Scoop and discard the scums and oil on the soup.
  • 6Add the Knorr chicken cube and chayote or papaya. Stir. Cover and cook for 5 minutes.
  • 7Put the malunggay and hot pepper leaves in the pot and pour the fish sauce in. Continue to cook for 2 minutes.
  • 8Transfer to a serving bowl. Serve.
